Bond Fields

The Bond type exposes the following members.

Fields
  NameDescription
Aromatic
Atoms are bonded by a delocalized bond of an aromatic system.
Dot
Atoms are not bonded.
Double
Atoms are bonded by two pairs of electrons.
DoubleAromatic
A double bond which is delocalised.
Down
Directional, single or aromatic bond (currently always single). The bond is relative to each endpoint such that the second endpoint is below the first or the first end point is above the second.
Implicit
Atoms are bonded by either a single or aromatic bond.
ImplicitAromatic
An implicit bond which is delocalised.
Quadruple
Atoms are bonded by four pairs of electrons.
Single
Atoms are bonded by a single pair of electrons.
Triple
Atoms are bonded by three pairs of electrons.
Up
Directional, single or aromatic bond (currently always single). The bond is relative to each endpoint such that the second endpoint is above the first or the first end point is below the second.
